Types of rice

Basmati. The BEST type of rice, it is a long grain, slender, amazing, fluffy white rice native to India, Pakistan and Nepal. Basmati can be found in plenty of Indian, Middle Eastern and Persian dishes, some include biryani, pilaf and stews.
Other types of rice are short-grain, medium-grain and long-grain, these diffirent types of rice are used in diffirent types of dishes despite being the same colour. For example. Short grain white rice is used for suhi, pnigiri and various japanese dishes, whilst medium-grain white rice is more commonly used in Middle Eastern, Korean and Spanish dishes, including paella, rissoto and fried rice.

Red rice

Red rice can be any of multiple varieties of rice that are high in anthocyanin, an antioxidant pigment that dyes the bran of the rice grain a reddish color.
The most widely available varieties of red rice include West African red rice, Bhutanese red rice, and Thai red rice.
Red rice is either sold whole-grain or partially milled to show off the red color, which turns the rice pink when cooked.
Whole-grain rice is rice that contains all three parts of the rice grain: endosperm, germ, and bran.
Whole-grain rice is usually called brown rice since the bran of most rice varieties is brown in color.

White rice

Many people consider white rice an “empty” or “bad” carb since it loses nutrients when the bran and germ are removed.
However, white rice is typically enriched with added nutrients such as iron and B vitamins.
So while brown rice does have more nutrients than white rice, white rice is still considered nutritious.

Black rice

Believe it or not, black rice exists and is used all across Asia.
Though it is black, it often turns dark purple once it is cooked, and it is described to have a mild, nutty taste, similar to brown rice.
It is a good source of antioxidants, iron and vitamins, giving it the title of healthiest rice in the world.
